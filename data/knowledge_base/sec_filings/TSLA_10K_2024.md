# Tesla, Inc. (TSLA) — 10-K Annual Report Excerpt (FY 2024)

## Business Overview
Tesla, Inc. designs, develops, manufactures, and sells electric vehicles (EVs), energy generation and storage systems, and related services. Automotive products include Model S, Model 3, Model Y, Model X, Cybertruck, and Semi. Energy products include Megapack, Powerwall, and Solar Roof. The company also develops autonomous driving technology (Full Self-Driving).

## Financial Highlights
- Total revenue: $97.7 billion (FY 2024), up 1% year-over-year
- Automotive revenue: $77.1 billion (79% of total)
- Energy generation and storage revenue: $10.4 billion, up 67% YoY
- Automotive gross margin: 18.2% (down from 19.4% in FY 2023) due to price reductions
- Net income: $7.1 billion; Diluted EPS: $2.04
- Operating cash flow: $13.3 billion
- Free cash flow: $3.6 billion
- Total vehicle deliveries: 1.79 million units

## Risk Factors
- Increasing competition in the global EV market from traditional automakers and new entrants
- Price sensitivity and impact of price reductions on margins
- Regulatory uncertainty around autonomous driving technology
- Dependence on government incentives and EV tax credits
- Supply chain risks for battery materials (lithium, cobalt, nickel)
- Manufacturing ramp challenges for new products (Cybertruck, next-gen platform)
- CEO Elon Musk's involvement in multiple ventures and public communications

## Growth Initiatives
Tesla is developing a next-generation vehicle platform targeting lower price points. The company continues to expand its Supercharger network (60,000+ connectors globally). Energy storage deployments reached record levels with Megapack demand exceeding supply. Full Self-Driving (Supervised) is available in North America with plans for global expansion. Tesla is also developing Optimus, a humanoid robot for manufacturing and household tasks.

## Manufacturing
Tesla operates Gigafactories in Fremont (California), Austin (Texas), Shanghai (China), Berlin (Germany), and Buffalo (New York). Shanghai Gigafactory is the highest-volume facility. Total installed annual vehicle production capacity exceeds 2.3 million units.
