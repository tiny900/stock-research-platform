# NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) — 10-K Annual Report Excerpt (FY 2025, ended Jan 2025)

## Business Overview
NVIDIA Corporation designs and manufactures graphics processing units (GPUs) and system-on-chip units. The company operates in two segments: Compute & Networking (data center GPUs, DGX systems, networking, automotive) and Graphics (GeForce GPUs, Quadro/RTX for professionals). NVIDIA's CUDA platform enables GPU-accelerated computing for AI, scientific computing, and data analytics.

## Financial Highlights
- Total revenue: $130.5 billion (FY 2025), up 114% year-over-year
- Data Center revenue: $115.2 billion (88% of total), up 142% YoY
- Gaming revenue: $11.4 billion
- Gross margin: 73.0%
- Operating income: $81.4 billion (62.4% operating margin)
- Net income: $72.9 billion; Diluted EPS: $2.94
- Operating cash flow: $64.1 billion

## AI and Data Center Dominance
NVIDIA's data center GPUs (H100, H200, Blackwell architecture) are the primary hardware for training and running large language models and AI applications. Major customers include hyperscale cloud providers (Microsoft Azure, Amazon AWS, Google Cloud), AI companies, and enterprises. The company's CUDA software ecosystem creates significant switching costs and competitive moat.

## Risk Factors
- Customer concentration — top customers represent a significant portion of revenue
- Geopolitical risks and US export restrictions on advanced chips to China
- Supply constraints for advanced semiconductor manufacturing (TSMC dependency)
- Potential competition from custom AI chips (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, AMD MI300)
- Rapid technology cycles requiring continued R&D investment
- Potential demand normalization after AI infrastructure buildout phase

## Product Roadmap
NVIDIA maintains an annual GPU architecture cadence. The Blackwell architecture (B100, B200, GB200) launched in 2024 with significant performance improvements for AI training and inference. The Vera Rubin architecture is planned for 2026. NVIDIA is also expanding into networking (InfiniBand, Ethernet switches) and software (NVIDIA AI Enterprise, Omniverse).
