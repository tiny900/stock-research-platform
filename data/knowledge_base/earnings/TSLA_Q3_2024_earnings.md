# Tesla, Inc. (TSLA) — Q3 2024 Earnings Call Summary

## Key Metrics
- Revenue: $25.2 billion (+8% YoY)
- EPS: $0.72 (+9% YoY)
- Automotive gross margin: 20.0% (improved from 18.7% in Q2)
- Total vehicle deliveries: 462,890 units (+6% YoY)
- Energy storage deployments: 6.9 GWh (record quarter)

## CEO Elon Musk Highlights
- Vehicle deliveries returned to growth trajectory after challenging first half
- Automotive margins improving through manufacturing cost reductions
- Energy storage business growing significantly and becoming a major revenue driver
- Full Self-Driving (Supervised) v12 showing substantial improvement with neural net approach
- Unveiled Cybercab (robotaxi) concept with planned sub-$30,000 price point
- Optimus robot development progressing toward limited production in 2025

## CFO Vaibhav Taneja Guidance
- Expects slight vehicle delivery growth in 2024 versus 2023
- Margins expected to continue improving with new manufacturing efficiencies
- Energy business on pace to be a $10+ billion annual revenue segment
- Capex expected between $10-11 billion for the full year
- New vehicle models expected to begin production in H1 2025

## Analyst Q&A Key Points
- Affordable model launch timeline pushed to H1 2025
- Supercharger network expansion resuming after earlier pause
- China demand stabilizing with competitive pricing and features
- Regulatory approvals for FSD remain uncertain across jurisdictions
- Insurance business growing and providing valuable driving data
- Megapack factory in Shanghai under construction

## Market Reaction
Stock surged approximately +22% following earnings, driven by better-than-expected margins and optimistic outlook on AI/robotaxi and energy business potential.
