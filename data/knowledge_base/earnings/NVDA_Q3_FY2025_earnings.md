# NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) — Q3 FY2025 Earnings Call Summary

## Key Metrics
- Revenue: $35.1 billion (+94% YoY, +17% QoQ)
- EPS: $0.81 (+103% YoY)
- Data Center revenue: $30.8 billion (+112% YoY)
- Gaming revenue: $3.3 billion (+15% YoY)
- Gross margin: 74.6%

## CEO Jensen Huang Highlights
- Demand for Hopper (H100/H200) GPUs continues to exceed supply
- Blackwell architecture production ramp began in Q3 with strong demand signals
- Every major cloud provider committed to Blackwell deployment
- AI inference demand growing rapidly as enterprises deploy AI in production
- Sovereign AI initiatives driving demand from government and national-scale projects
- NVIDIA software ecosystem (CUDA, TensorRT, NIM) deepening competitive moat

## CFO Colette Kress Guidance
- Q4 FY2025 revenue expected approximately $37.5 billion (+/- 2%)
- Data Center revenue expected to continue growing driven by Blackwell ramp
- Gross margins expected to moderate to low-70s% during Blackwell ramp-up
- Supply expected to improve throughout FY2026
- Continued R&D investment in next-generation architectures

## Analyst Q&A Key Points
- Blackwell demand from cloud providers is "insane" (Jensen Huang quote)
- Networking revenue (InfiniBand, Ethernet) growing as a percentage of Data Center
- China revenue stabilized at reduced levels due to export restrictions
- Automotive AI computing platform (DRIVE) growing with partnerships
- No meaningful competition from custom AI chips disrupting NVIDIA's market share
- Enterprise AI adoption still in early stages with long runway for growth

## Market Reaction
Stock gained approximately +3% after hours but traded volatile. Record revenue beat expectations, though some investors took profits given the massive year-to-date rally. Analysts broadly reiterated buy ratings citing continued AI infrastructure spending cycle.
