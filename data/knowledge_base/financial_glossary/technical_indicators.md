# Technical Indicators Glossary

## RSI (Relative Strength Index)
RSI is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of price changes on a scale of 0 to 100. Developed by J. Welles Wilder Jr. in 1978. RSI above 70 indicates overbought conditions (potential sell signal), while RSI below 30 indicates oversold conditions (potential buy signal). The standard calculation period is 14 days. RSI is calculated as: RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS)), where RS = Average Gain / Average Loss over the period.

## MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two exponential moving averages (EMAs) of a security's price. The MACD line is calculated by subtracting the 26-period EMA from the 12-period EMA. A 9-period EMA of the MACD line is called the signal line. When MACD crosses above the signal line, it generates a bullish signal; when it crosses below, it generates a bearish signal. The MACD histogram represents the difference between MACD and the signal line.

## SMA (Simple Moving Average)
SMA is the arithmetic mean of a security's price over a specified number of periods. Common periods include 20-day (short-term), 50-day (medium-term), and 200-day (long-term). When the price is above the SMA, it indicates an uptrend; when below, a downtrend. The golden cross occurs when a shorter-period SMA crosses above a longer-period SMA (bullish), while the death cross is the opposite (bearish).

## EMA (Exponential Moving Average)
EMA is a type of moving average that gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive to new information than the SMA. The weighting multiplier is calculated as 2 / (period + 1). EMA reacts faster to price changes than SMA, which makes it popular among short-term traders. Like SMA, price above EMA suggests bullish conditions and price below suggests bearish conditions.

## Bollinger Bands
Bollinger Bands consist of a middle band (20-period SMA) and two outer bands set 2 standard deviations above and below the middle band. They measure market volatility. When bands narrow (squeeze), it suggests low volatility and potential breakout. When price touches the upper band, the security may be overbought; touching the lower band may indicate oversold. The %B indicator shows where price is relative to the bands: %B = (Price - Lower Band) / (Upper Band - Lower Band).

## Volume
Volume represents the total number of shares or contracts traded during a given period. High volume confirms price trends — a price move on high volume is more significant than one on low volume. Volume typically increases during breakouts and decreases during consolidations. On-Balance Volume (OBV) is a cumulative indicator that adds volume on up days and subtracts on down days.

## Support and Resistance
Support is a price level where buying interest is strong enough to prevent further decline. Resistance is a price level where selling pressure prevents further advance. These levels are identified through historical price action, round numbers, moving averages, and Fibonacci retracement levels. A break above resistance or below support often leads to significant price moves.

## Volatility
Volatility measures the degree of variation in a security's price over time. Historical volatility is calculated from past price data using standard deviation. Annualized volatility = daily volatility × √252 (trading days per year). Higher volatility means higher risk but also higher potential returns. The VIX index measures expected market volatility.

## Maximum Drawdown
Maximum drawdown measures the largest peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value before a new peak is reached. It is expressed as a percentage. For example, if a portfolio's value drops from $100 to $70 before recovering, the maximum drawdown is 30%. It is a key risk metric used to evaluate the worst-case scenario for an investment strategy.

## Golden Cross / Death Cross
A golden cross occurs when a short-term moving average (typically 50-day SMA) crosses above a long-term moving average (typically 200-day SMA), signaling a potential bullish trend. A death cross is the opposite — when the short-term MA crosses below the long-term MA, signaling a potential bearish trend. These are lagging indicators and work best in trending markets.
