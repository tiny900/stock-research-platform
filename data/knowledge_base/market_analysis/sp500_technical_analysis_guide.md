# Technical Analysis Guide for S&P 500 Stocks

## How to Read Technical Indicators Together

### Trend Confirmation Framework
No single indicator should be used in isolation. A robust technical analysis combines multiple indicator types:

1. **Trend Indicators** (SMA, EMA): Determine the direction — is price in an uptrend, downtrend, or range?
2. **Momentum Indicators** (RSI, MACD): Determine the strength — is the trend gaining or losing steam?
3. **Volatility Indicators** (Bollinger Bands): Determine the environment — is the market calm or volatile?
4. **Volume Confirmation**: Validate — does volume support the price movement?

### Signal Strength Scoring
- **Strong Bullish**: Price above SMA + RSI 50-70 + MACD bullish crossover + Bollinger within bands trending up + Above-average volume
- **Moderate Bullish**: Price above SMA + RSI 40-60 + MACD positive but no recent crossover
- **Neutral**: Mixed signals across indicators, price near SMA, RSI around 50
- **Moderate Bearish**: Price below SMA + RSI 30-50 + MACD below signal line
- **Strong Bearish**: Price below SMA + RSI below 30 + MACD bearish crossover + Bollinger band breakdown + High volume

### Common Pitfalls
- **Overbought ≠ Sell**: RSI above 70 in a strong uptrend can stay overbought for extended periods. Only consider selling on RSI divergence (price making new highs while RSI makes lower highs).
- **Whipsaw Signals**: In ranging/choppy markets, moving average crossovers generate frequent false signals. Use wider stops or wait for confirmation.
- **Timeframe Alignment**: Always check multiple timeframes. A daily chart may show bullish signals while the weekly chart is bearish — the higher timeframe generally dominates.

### Sector-Specific Considerations
- **Technology stocks**: Tend to trend strongly; momentum indicators work well. Higher beta means wider Bollinger Bands.
- **Utilities/Staples**: Lower volatility, mean-reverting behavior; RSI overbought/oversold levels are more reliable.
- **Financial stocks**: Sensitive to interest rate expectations; fundamental catalysts often override technicals.
- **Energy stocks**: Correlated with oil prices; watch commodity charts alongside equity charts.

### Risk Management Integration
- Set stop-loss orders at key technical levels (below support, below SMA)
- Position size based on volatility (wider Bollinger Bands = smaller position)
- Take partial profits at resistance levels
- Move stop-loss to breakeven after a meaningful move in your favor
